# Example output: ICP Reality Check

Sample data is fictional. Use this as a shape reference for the expected output, not as a benchmark.

## Input summary

Stated ICP:

- B2B SaaS companies
- 50-500 employees
- Marketing, RevOps or founder buyer
- $18k target ACV
- 60-day sales cycle

90-day input:

- 420 paid leads from Google Ads and LinkedIn Ads
- 38 sales-qualified leads
- 9 opportunities
- 2 closed-won deals
- CRM fields: source, company size, role, stage, disqualification reason, closed-won value

## Findings

| Finding | Evidence | Lead quality impact | Recommended action | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| The stated ICP is too broad below 100 employees. | 50-99 employee companies produced 41% of leads, 8% of SQLs, 0 opportunities. | High lead volume is hiding low sales fit. | Exclude 50-99 employees from paid prospecting for 30 days. Keep them in nurture if acquisition cost stays low. | High |
| RevOps and Marketing Ops roles are stronger than founders. | RevOps / Marketing Ops produced 31% of leads, 58% of SQLs and 67% of opportunities. Founder leads produced 26% of leads and 1 opportunity. | Budget is split across roles with different buying readiness. | Split founder-led campaigns from ops-led campaigns. Score ops titles higher. | Medium |
| The "AI report template" asset attracts research behavior. | Template downloads had high form completion, but 62% were marked "student / freelancer / no budget" by sales. | The asset creates activity without pipeline. | Use the template as top-of-funnel nurture. Stop using it as the main conversion for high-intent campaigns. | High |
| Closed-won deals share an implementation pain. | Both closed-won accounts mention "manual campaign QA" and "weekly reporting load" in sales notes. | Current targeting underuses the pain that actually converts. | Add implementation pain to ad copy, landing page proof and qualification questions. | Medium |

## Decision

Tighten the paid ICP for the next 30 days:

- Active paid ICP: B2B SaaS, 100-500 employees, RevOps / Marketing Ops / CMO
- Secondary nurture segment: founders and 50-99 employee companies
- Temporary exclusion: students, freelancers, agencies, companies without paid acquisition spend

Do not scale spend until SQL rate and opportunity rate improve in the narrowed segment.

## Approval needed

- Confirm whether 50-99 employee companies can be excluded from paid campaigns.
- Confirm whether the lead magnet CTA can move from "download template" to a stronger diagnostic offer.
- Confirm which CRM field should store "implementation pain" for future reporting.
